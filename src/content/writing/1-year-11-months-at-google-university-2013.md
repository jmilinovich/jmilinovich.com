I feel fortunate to have spent nearly two years as a student at Google, the most forward-thinking company in the world. Surrounded by incredibly talented people solving difficult problems, I experienced a culture centered on creativity, learning, and personal growth.

Today marks my final day at Google. While deeply sad to leave, I am "uncomfortably excited" about the future. The relationships, experience, and skills I gained have given me confidence to pursue my dream of becoming a startup co-founder.

Some of my most stimulating conversations happened over meals at Beta, Charlie's, or Big Table. The culture was distinctly "Googley" -- unique viewpoints, analytical thinking, and creativity were palpable throughout campus.

Google empowers employees to work at incredible scale across all levels. I launched products, built global programs, patented technologies, and managed partnerships affecting tens of millions. I completed 20% projects, attended author talks, learned Linux, provided support, and filed bugs.

Google's education investment stands as my favorite perk. I completed 100+ hours in Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, meditation, strategic thinking, creative thinking, negotiations, data visualization, and authentic relationships.

Thank you, Google.
