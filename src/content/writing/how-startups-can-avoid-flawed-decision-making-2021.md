---
title: "The courage to course correct"
date: "2021-01-02"
---

I built Aesthetic to replace designers with software. Then I almost killed it by solving the wrong problem.

We hired creative directors and contract designers. Within months, dozens of startups wanted our help. But our software wasn't ready. We hired to handle work manually. One year later, we'd become exactly what we'd set out to disrupt.

Michael Porter defines strategy as "achieving sustainable competitive advantage by preserving what is distinctive." When I measured our choices against this standard, I saw how far we'd strayed.

We reset. Fired our full-time team. Turned designers into contractors and eventually sold them the service business. We got to refocus on our original vision.

Three navigational lights: Your mission (the change you'll make), your vision (what you'll build in two years), your strategy (how you'll prove it works). For every decision, ask: "How does this serve our strategy?"

Don't let your next "just for now" become a "that's who we are." But if it does -- have the courage to change it.
