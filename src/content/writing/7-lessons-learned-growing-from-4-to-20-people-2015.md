---
title: "Small is fast"
date: "2015-05-24"
---

At 4 people, we moved like lightning. At 20, we felt stuck in mud.

The real problem is math. Four people need 6 connections to stay in sync. Twenty people need 190. Most companies respond by adding meetings. You can't solve exponential growth with linear tools.

What works: Split information into need-to-know and nice-to-know. At four people this didn't matter. At twenty it's survival. Most decisions don't need everyone.

Your job as CEO transforms. At four you're a player. At twenty you're a referee. Stop solving known problems -- find unknown ones. Say no ruthlessly. Build processes before you need them. Your mood is contagious, and the infection rate doubles with each hire.

What worked: Write daily updates instead of meetings. Kill update-sharing meetings. Build public dashboards. Create clear ownership for every project and metric.

Speed comes from knowing where you're going, not from rushing to get there. A smaller team moving in the right direction beats a bigger team running in circles.
