title: "Organizational OODA Loops"
date: "2024-02-23"
---

Scaling meeting time sublinear to team size. I joined Canva just under a year ago, and my biggest learning has been how to build large (150+ people), healthy, and high-functioning organizations while being on the opposite side of the world from my colleagues.

The OODA loop was invented by US Air Force Colonel John Boyd to explain how fighter pilots could gain a competitive edge in dogfights. This iterative process (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) works well within fast-scaling companies.

**Observe:** Direct observation (dashboards, documents, meetings) and indirect observations (discussions with teammates, escalations).

**Orient:** Reflect on and assess relevant information. Cross-reference prior observations, gather verified data, user interviews. Understand broader implications.

**Decide:** Internal decisions (made quickly by few people) vs External decisions (require broader stakeholder sign-off). Always ratified in writing.

**Act:** Once a clear decision has been made and documented, the recommended path is carried out.

The most critical cadence: Fortnightly project check-ins -- fifteen 15-minute back-to-back meetings with leads of major project areas. Each fills out a doc 24 hours ahead calling out progress, plans, and blockers. Meetings start and end on time; canceled if no critical discussion items.

Move fast and keep receipts.
