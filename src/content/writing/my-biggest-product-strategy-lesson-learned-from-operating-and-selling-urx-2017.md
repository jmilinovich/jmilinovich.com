---
title: "URX retro: business model-vision fit"
date: "2017-05-02"
---

On May 2, 2016, we announced Pinterest's acquisition of URX. My biggest learning: the importance of aligning your vision with your business model.

URX was one of the first mobile deep linking companies. We built an SDK and click server, then pivoted to mobile retargeting, raised a $3.1M seed and $12M Series A. To scale, we became an ad network -- which misaligned our business model with our vision of contextual app discovery.

When you're operating a media business, your success metrics are very different from a discovery/search company. We had to balance end-users with publishers and advertisers. By the time we realized the wrong business model, it was too late to change.

To check alignment, regularly ask: What products could we build? Who benefits? What's the ideal revenue model? Then compare near-term execution with long-term mission. The most successful companies (Google, Airbnb, Pinterest, Netflix) expand upon the same customer pain point throughout their lives.

If misaligned: with an established business, consider changing the mission. With a nascent business, validate a new business model with customers.
